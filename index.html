Daniel Posmik

I am a PhD student in Biostatistics at the Brown University School of Public Health, advised by Ani Eloyan. My research focuses on assumption-lean statistical methods for high-dimensional biomedical data, with emphasis on manifold learning, network geometry, and shape analysis applied to brain imaging (tau PET, fMRI, MRI).

Previously, I studied at the University of Cincinnati and spent time at UChicago before arriving at Brown. I hold a BA in [Your Degree] from the University of Cincinnati.

Research Interests

My primary research interest lies at the intersection of manifold learning, network geometry, and neuroimaging. My current work extends principal manifold estimation (PME) to hierarchical imaging datasets, with prior work on tau PET bias-corrected principal curves and GNN-based protein–protein interaction prediction.
