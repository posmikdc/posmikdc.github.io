Daniel Posmik

Hi! My name is Daniel (He/Him) and I am a PhD student in the Brown University Department of Biostatistics, advised by Dr. Ani Eloyan. I am drawn to large-scale inference problems that involve geometry, shape, and networks. I am most interested in developing manifold learning techniques for biomedical image data analysis, particularly for neuroimaging. In my free-time, I am an avid outdoorsman, cyclist, and lover of dogs. Research Interests

My primary research interest concerns manifold learning for neuroimaging applications. My current work extends principal manifold estimation (PME) to hierarchical imaging datasets, with a focus on multimodal (i.e. combining PET and fMRI) inference on neurodegenerative diseases. I have previously worked on causal inference, specifically causal mediation analysis.
