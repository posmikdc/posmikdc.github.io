Daniel Posmik

I am a PhD student in the Brown University Department of Biostatistics, advised by Dr. Ani Eloyan. I am drawn to large-scale inference problems that involve geometry, shapes, and networks. I am most interested in developing manifold learning techniques for biomedical image data analysis, particularly for neuroimaging.

In my free-time, I am an avid bikepacker, urbanist, and lover of dogs. If you are interested in having a chat on any of the above, please do not hesitate to reach out to me at daniel_posmik[at]brown[dot]edu.

Research Interests

My primary research interest concerns the application of manifold learning to neuroimaging applications. My current work extends principal manifold estimation to hierarchical imaging datasets, with a focus on principled multimodal (i.e. combining PET and fMRI) inference on neurodegenerative diseases. I have previously worked on causal inference, specifically causal mediation analysis.
